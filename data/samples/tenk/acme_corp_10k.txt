ACME CORPORATION
FORM 10-K — FISCAL YEAR ENDED DECEMBER 31, 2025

## Business Overview

ACME Corporation manufactures industrial automation controllers and cloud-connected
edge gateways. Approximately 62% of revenue derives from North American automotive
and semiconductor capital equipment customers. We depend on three contract manufacturers
in Malaysia, Vietnam, and Mexico for final assembly.

## Risk Factors

We face substantial concentration risk: our top five customers accounted for 48% of
net revenue in fiscal 2025. Loss of any major customer could materially harm results.

Our products incorporate custom ASICs sourced from a single foundry partner in Taiwan.
Geopolitical tension, export controls, or natural disasters affecting that region could
interrupt supply for six months or longer.

We rely on a legacy ERP platform hosted in a single U.S. data center. A prolonged
outage could delay order fulfillment and financial close processes.

Foreign exchange fluctuations, particularly USD versus MYR and TWD, may reduce gross
margin when we cannot pass through cost increases.

## Management's Discussion and Analysis

Gross margin declined 310 basis points year-over-year due to input cost inflation and
expedited freight. Management implemented a 12% headcount reduction in Q3 2025 but
maintains elevated R&D spend for next-generation safety-certified controllers.

Liquidity remains adequate with $420M cash, but covenant headroom under our revolving
credit facility narrows if EBITDA falls below $180M for two consecutive quarters.

## Legal Proceedings

The SEC is investigating our revenue recognition practices for multi-year service bundles
sold in 2023–2024. We cannot predict timing or outcome; adverse findings could require
restatement and trigger customer contract renegotiations.

A class action alleging misleading statements about supply chain resilience was filed
in the Northern District of California in January 2026.

## Cybersecurity

In November 2025 we detected unauthorized access to a staging environment containing
non-production customer telemetry. Forensics are ongoing. We have not determined whether
production systems or personally identifiable information were affected.

We are enhancing zero-trust network segmentation and MFA coverage for privileged accounts.

## Regulatory Exposure

New EU machinery regulation (EU) 2023/1230 requires additional functional safety
documentation by March 2027. Non-compliance could block shipments to European OEMs
representing 19% of revenue.

U.S. CHIPS Act incentives require domestic content thresholds we may not meet without
requalifying suppliers.

## Supply Chain Dependencies

Critical passive components and power modules are sole-sourced from two Japanese vendors.
Lead times extended from 14 to 38 weeks during fiscal 2025. We hold 90 days of safety
stock for tier-1 SKUs but not for legacy platforms scheduled for end-of-life in 2026.
